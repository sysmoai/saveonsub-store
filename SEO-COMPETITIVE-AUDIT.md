# SAVEONSUB — Brutal SEO Competitive Audit & Keyword Strategy

> Real-time competitive research from live BD SERP surveys across 7 market sprints. Every gap here is being fixed in `build_*.py`. Keyword universe generated by `build_keywords.py` → 1,849 keywords across 64 products.

## 1. The BD competitor SERP landscape (who ranks for "X price in bangladesh")

**Established players we must outrank:** subscriptionsbd.net · subscriptionsmartbd.com · shopinbd.com · tech-haat.com · digitalproductsbd.com · aisubscriptionbd.com · zamve.com · martsoon.com · aistorebd.com · netflixmartbd.net · josscreative.com · seotoolbd.com · premiumbangladesh.com · trustedsubscriptionbd.com · dhakadigitalxyz.com.

**Their SEO strengths:** domain age, thousands of indexed product URLs, per-product pages titled exactly "X price in Bangladesh", bKash/Nagad in title tags, aggressive volume.

**Their SEO WEAKNESSES (our opening — every one is a ranking wedge):**
1. Thin content — most product pages are 100-200 words, price + buy button, no depth.
2. Zero Product/Offer/FAQ schema on most → no rich results, no AI-answer eligibility.
3. No honest comparison content — they never publish competitor prices or risk labels.
4. No llms.txt / AIO optimization — invisible to ChatGPT/Perplexity answers.
5. Weak/absent Bangla keyword coverage in structured markup.
6. No internal linking depth, no topic clusters, no informational guides.
7. Often fake "lifetime" / fake-review spam → E-E-A-T weakness Google increasingly punishes.

## 2. Our keyword universe (per product — `build_keywords.py`)

Every product now carries a structured `seo` block: **primary** ("X price in bangladesh"), **short_tail** (10: bd price, bkash, nagad, subscription bangladesh…), **long_tail** (12: "buy X in bangladesh with bkash", "cheap X subscription bangladesh", "X shared account bangladesh", "X for students bangladesh"…), **informational** (4: "is X worth it", "X vs free tier", "X review bangladesh", "how much is X"), **bangla** (5 native-script), **transliteration** (3: "X dam koto", "X kivabe kinbo"). Avg 28 keywords/product.

## 3. On-page SEO gaps found → fixes applied

| # | Gap | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Product titles used generic "X Price in Bangladesh — ৳Y" but no keyword variants captured | title = exact primary keyword; meta keywords tag added; H1 keyword-aligned |
| 2 | No `<meta name="keywords">` (minor but zero-cost) + no keyword-rich body section | inject "Commonly searched as" keyword block + meta keywords per product |
| 3 | No Bangla keyword coverage in crawlable text | Bangla + transliteration keywords rendered in a labeled block per product |
| 4 | No hreflang (bn-BD / en) — BD bilingual signal missing | add hreflang alternate + x-default on every page |
| 5 | Thin product-page body (< competitor depth on some) | market table + keyword block + expanded FAQ lift word count above competitors |
| 6 | Internal linking shallow — products didn't link to matching guides | product → relevant guide links (SEO-3) |
| 7 | llms.txt lacked per-product keyword phrasing (AIO) | keywords fed into llms.txt product lines |
| 8 | Breadcrumb used bare product name | keep (correct), but ensure BreadcrumbList schema present (already ✅) |
